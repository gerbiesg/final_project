Chevrolet Camaro: A Brief History

Launched for the 1967 model year, the Camaro was Chevrolet's answer to the Ford Mustang. Across six generations it evolved from raw pony-car to modern performance coupe, all while retaining its rear-wheel-drive layout and unmistakable presence. The first-gen set the tone with aggressive styling and big-block V8s. The '70s brought a longer, lower second-gen that became an icon of the muscle-car era. Lightweight third-gens entered the 1980s with fuel injection and better handling. The fourth-gen embraced aerodynamics and LS-series power. After a hiatus, the retro-inspired fifth-gen returned in 2010, leading to today's sixth-gen: lighter, sharper and track-ready, cementing Camaro's legacy as an American performance legend.
