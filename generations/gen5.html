Fifth Generation Camaro (2010 - 2015)

Launched in spring 2009 as a 2010 model, the fifth-generation Camaro rode GM's Zeta platform—finalized by Holden in Australia—and was built in Oshawa, Ontario, its retro body borrowing key 1969 cues. Coupés in LS, LT and SS trims arrived first with a 312-hp 3.6 L V-6 or, in the SS, a 426-hp LS3 V-8 (400-hp L99 with the automatic); an RS package added 20-inch wheels and halo-ring headlamps. A fuel-sipping 2LS variant, a convertible (from 2011), and long-delayed export versions followed, while 2010's design earned World Car Design of the Year honors. The 2012 lineup marked the Camaro's 45th anniversary with refreshed styling, a 323-hp “LFX” V-6, chassis tweaks for the SS, and the 580-hp supercharged ZL1 (also offered as a 2013 convertible). A 2014 facelift brought a slimmer nose, LED-equipped RS package, and the track-focused Z/28 packing a 505-hp 7.0 L LS7 V-8.
