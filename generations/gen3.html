Third Generation Camaro (1982 - 1992)

Built for the 1982-1992 model years, the third-generation Camaro was about 500 lb lighter than its predecessor and brought the line into the modern era with fuel injection, a 700R4 four-speed automatic, a five-speed manual, hatchback styling, 14-16-inch wheels, and a standard four-cylinder (soon replaced by a 2.8 L V6). The high-performance IROC-Z arrived in 1985, the same year the 5.0 L V8 gained tuned-port injection; federal rules added a center high-mount brake light in 1986, followed by a convertible's return and the 5.7 L L98 V8 option in 1987. Subsequent highlights included the track-focused 1LE package (1988), the subtler B4C “police” package (1991), and stripe-and-badge anniversary editions for 1987's 20th and 1992's 25th celebrations, while the IROC-Z ran through 1990.
