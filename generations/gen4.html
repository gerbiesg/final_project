Fourth Generation Camaro (1993 - 2002)

Produced from 1993 to 2002, the fourth-generation Camaro rode an updated F-body but kept its classic formula: 2+2 coupé or (from 1994) convertible, rear-wheel drive, and pushrod V-6 or V-8 power. Early cars used a 3.4 L V-6, replaced by a 3.8 L unit in 1995, while the Z28 carried the 275 hp LT1 5.7 L V-8 with optional all-speed traction control, a six-speed T-56 manual, or standard 4L60-E automatic; anti-lock brakes were standard across the line. A limited 1996-97 SS run offered the 330 hp LT4, though most SS and Z28 models kept the LT1 until 1998, when a facelift introduced Corvett's 305 hp all-aluminum LS1, along with suspension, wheel, and aerodynamic upgrades for SS versions. Interior updates arrived for 1997, and a 35th-anniversary edition capped the 2002 finale. Despite special variants such as the B4C police package, declining sports-coupé demand ended F-body production in 2002, with the Camaro returning on an all-new platform for 2010.
